Seowoo is an interdisciplinary design researcher who explores designerly ways of engaging with emerging technologies to address sociotechnical issues. In particular, she is interested in new forms of agency enabled by data and their intra-actions as fluid assemblages in complex systems.

At the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Science and Technologies Studies (STS), her research hovers around theories of posthuman, alien phenomenology, complexity, and technological mediation. The current interest areas include:

1. Developing posthumanist design approaches/strategies for intelligent agents and data-driven systems
- e.g. How AI system co-evolves their own perception, agencies, and trajectories with people
- e.g.2 Design strategies to bring nonhumans as more proactive agents in our urban fabric
2. Developing alternative modes of data collection and sensemaking for AI/ML
- e.g. Designing scalable design probes
- e.g.2 How things gather contextual data for design ethnography
- e.g.3 How environmental data can help gain a new understanding of more-than-humans
3. Designing tools for designers/developers who design for and with AI
